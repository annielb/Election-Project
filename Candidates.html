    • The candidates for the two main parties (Democratic and Republican) are Hillary Clinton and Donald Trump.

    Hillary Clinton

    Hillary Clinton is the nominee for the Democratic party in the 2016 Election. In fact, she is the first woman to ever be nominated for a major political party in the United States. She is the wife of former U.S. president Bill Clinton, and held the position of First Lady of the United States for 8 years during Bill Clinton's two terms as president from 1993 to 2001. After that, she became the junior United States Senator from New York from 2001-2009. During her time as a senator, she ran for the 2008 Presidential Election, but lost the Democratic nomination to candidate Barack Obama, who is currently the president of the United States. She served as the 67th United States Secretary of State from 2009 to 2013.

    Donald Trump

    Donald Trump is the nominee for the Republican Party in the 2016 Election. He is the chairman and CEO of the Trump Organization, an international conglomerate that serves as a principal holding company for his business ventures and investments. He is also famously known for his reality television show The Apprentice, a competition where the winner would receive a high-level job in one of Trump's commercial enterprises. Trump has considered running for office many times, but has never been nominated by a party until this Presidential race.
